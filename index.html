In June 2019, my partner and I decided to move to Alaska.

Why Alaska?

Certain opportunities have a way of feeling like destiny. We were preparing for a post graduation vacation to Costa Rica in March, and on our way out of the house I remembered to hit "submit" on a long shot job application for biometrician position in Fairbanks, Alaska with the Fish & Game division of Wildlife Conservation. A statistical consultant who gets to jump out of helicopters and radio collar wild animals seemed like a job that 10-year-old me dreamed into existence. Two months later we were driving the Alaska Highway with everything we owned (and a dog) in a pickup camper on the back of my old truck.

Now, when I'm not fishing or skijoring, I'm developing sampling methods and conducting statistical analysis of resulting data to meet the goals of Alaska's wildlife management team. Our work is based on the principles of sustainable harvest, which requires a scientific understanding of population demographics, status and trends.

As a member of the biometrics team, I have been tasked with everything from radio collaring wolves in -20F tundra to creating a geospatial moose abundance model and accompanying Shiny application. I also created and administrate the biometrics Github organization, though most of our repositories are private.

My experience at OSU strengthened my foundation in statistics, but more importantly it opened the door to my true passion, data science. I spent my master's thesis project teaching myself machine learning alogrithms, creating an alogirithm selection tool that evaluated which would most accurately predict NBA shot make probabilities.
